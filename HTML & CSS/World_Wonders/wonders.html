Wonders of the World!

There are so many wonders around the world that you MUST VISIT !!

Great Pyramid of Giza

  • Location: Giza
  • Built in 2560 BCE

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex. It is the only Seven Wonders of the Ancient World that survived till today.

Great Wall of China

  • Location: China
  • Built in 700 BCE

The Great Wall of China is built to protect China against the raids and invasions of barbarians from the north . The Great Wall was built in several different eras and was further improved during 220–206 BC by Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China, in the Qin Dynasty. The current Great Wall is mostly remains from the Ming Dynasty.

Petra

  • Location: Jordan
  • Built in 312 BCE

Petra is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan. It is also known as the "Rose City" due to the pink color of the stone which the monument was carved out from. It is also featured in the film : Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade

The Colosseum

  • Location: Rome, Italy
  • Built in 80 CE

The Colosseum is the largest amphitheatre ever built. It could hold 80,000 spectators and was used for entertainment, including gladiatorial battles , animal hunts, executions, and dramas.

Chichen Itza

  • Location: Yucatán, Mexico
  • Built in 600 CE

Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people. It was an important Yucatan cultural zone from the 10th to the 15th century. It is now of high archaeological value for people to study about the Mayan culture.

Machu Picchu

  • Location: Cuzco Region, Peru
  • Built in 1450 CE

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel built on a mountain. It was constructed as an estate for the Inca emperor Pachacuti. It is the icon of Inca civilization. It has three primary architectures within the site, which are the Intihuatana, the Temple of the Sun, and the Room of the Three Windows.

Taj Mahal

  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Built in 1643 CE

Taj Mahal is commissioned by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan , to house the tomb of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The monument includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens. Its estimated cost is around 32 million rupees. The emperor employed approximately 20,000 artisans to build it.

Christ the Redeemer

  • Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
  • Built in 1931 CE

Christ the Redeemer is 30 metres tall, excluding the pedestal. The arms stretch 28 metres wide. The statue weighs 635 metric tons , and is located at the peak of the Corcovado mountain, overlooking the city of Rio de Janeiro, which symbolizes Christianity across the world
